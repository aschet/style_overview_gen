# PREFIX

Composition: A waist-up portrait of a 40-year-old Italian woman seated at a wooden dining table. Her body is angled slightly toward the viewer in a relaxed posture. She has black hair falling over her shoulders and wears a plain T-shirt. Her expression is calm as she actively drinks from a glass of beer, tipping it to her lips.

# Painting: Oil Paint

Medium: An intense oil painting defined by high-relief impasto ridges and a rich linseed-oil gloss finish. The medium showcases fluid wet-on-wet paint blending, heavy palette knife strokes, and deep canvas tooth. Features a complex, varied surface sheen that directly contrasts thick, opaque paint volume against thin, slick translucent glazes.

# Painting: Watercolor

Medium: A fluid watercolor painting relying entirely on absolute transparency and granulating pigments on rough, heavy cold-press paper. Characterized by blooming capillary edges, pooling pigment boundaries, and soft wet-on-wet bleeding. Features dry-brush scraping over the paper peaks, leaving a fragmented, completely weightless, and porous texture.

# Painting: Gouache

Medium: A dense, ultra-matte gouache painting with a dry, heavily chalky finish on thick archival cardstock texture. Opaque layers feature a distinct powdery pigment bloom and micro-fissures from rapid drying. Visible dry-brush scraping reveals a chalk-like texture, with raised matte paint ridges along overlapping brush tracks. The surface is entirely non-reflective, emphasizing velvety, chalky solids and crisp, hand-painted structural shapes.

# Painting: Encaustic

Medium: A heavily layered encaustic painting built from thick, fused coats of a pigmented wax medium. The artwork features a foggy, translucent depth with buried layers visible beneath the surface. The wax surface is carved, gouged, and scraped, displaying rugged incisions, raw pigment sediment, and smooth, heat-fused fluid flows with a distinct, tactile satin luster.

# Drawing: Ink

Medium: A stark monochrome ink drawing defined by high-density carbon black fluid and sharp, fine-line scratching. The heavy black ink pools and saturates deep into raw, fibrous rice paper, creating high-contrast silhouettes, natural bleeding edges, micro-feathering, and expressive, variable-width brush strokes.

# Drawing: Charcoal

Medium: A gritty, smoky charcoal drawing on heavily textured, high-tooth rag paper. Features deep, matte, velvety blacks from a dense compressed charcoal medium contrasting with loose, soft smoky charcoal gradients. The surface is raw and unstable, showing subtle light-erased highlights, loose charcoal dust fallout, and a rough texture.

# Drawing: Colored Pencil

Medium: A tight, highly controlled colored pencil drawing featuring dense, heavily burnished wax-based color that completely fills the paper pits to a hard, waxy sheen. Intricate directional hatching tracks and fine-point sharp scoring marks create sharp, un-smudged, crisp edges and multi-layered optical color mixtures.

# Drawing: Pastel

Medium: A loose, expressive soft pastel drawing with a crumbly, heavily saturated pigment pileup. The velvety surface catches the tooth of dense pastel paper to its maximum capacity, showing vibrant, powdery particulate fallout, softly blended paper smudges, and sharp, dry strokes from hard pastel edges.

# Printmaking: Woodcut

Medium: A bold graphic woodcut relief print pressed with heavy ink onto handmade paper. The style is defined strictly by high-contrast ink-stamped block shapes, clean chiseled negative space, and thick ink-squeeze ridges along the stamped edges. It showcases the stark, blocky perimeters of a traditional hand-carved printmaking plate.

# Printmaking: Lithograph

Medium: A classic lithograph print displaying greasy fluid washes and rich, velvety crayon textures. The print showcases the ultra-fine, organic grain texture of traditional planographic printmaking, subtle ink-rolling variations, and continuous, soft stippled tonal transitions.

# Printmaking: Screenprint

Medium: A bold, graphic screenprint featuring ultra-flat fields of heavy, opaque ink with noticeable off-contact ink ridges. A subtle graphic mesh-pattern weave texture is visible in large color fields, accompanied by slight registration misalignment gaps and sharp ink-track perimeters.

# Printmaking: Monotype

Medium: An authentic monotype print capturing a distinct hand-pulled graphic texture. The image features a fine, speckled ink grain that allows the organic cream-colored paper tooth to show through from behind the ink. The color transitions display a characteristic stippled texture from a brayer roller, while the details show soft, hand-drawn grease crayon or oil pastel marks. The flat fields of color exhibit a pressed plate quality with subtle, uneven ink opacity and soft, non-liquid edge boundaries.

# Photography: Gelatin Silver

Medium: A commanding gelatin silver darkroom photograph featuring deep, velvety photographic shadows and luminous, metallic highlights. The image explicitly displays heavy, pronounced, and highly visible analog silver halide grain clumping throughout the mid-tones, capturing continuous gray scales and the rich, subtle surface sheen of a traditional fiber-based baryta print.

# Photography: Chromogenic

Medium: An authentic chromogenic analog color photograph featuring a pronounced, highly visible film grain structure with a natural dye-cloud texture that mirrors classic darkroom grain clumping. The image showcases rich analog color tonality and realistic lens aberrations.

# Digital: Vector Graphic

Medium: A flawless digital vector graphic defined by pristine, mathematically perfect Bezier curves and razor-sharp geometric anchor points. Features absolute flat color blocks completely devoid of grain, texture, or aliasing artifacts, preserving immaculate, resolution-independent digital precision.

# Mixed Media: Collage

Medium: A chaotic, high-contrast mixed media collage defined by dense layering, transparent overlays, and aggressive material clashes. The artwork features integrated semi-transparent layers, frosted filmic textures, and translucent parchment overlays with printed typography that creates a ghosted, multi-layered depth. The subject is fractured and reassembled using a jarring mix of torn high-gloss printed fragments, rigid ribbed cardboard textures, and aged newspaper clippings showing heavy halftone dots. Heavy, tactile interventions cut across the composition—including thick paint slashes, ink splatters, and smudged dark gradients. Segments are visually bound together with translucent adhesive tape textures and ink-stamped graphical elements. The composition forces extreme textural contrast between matte, powdery surfaces and glossy photographic fragments, complete with physical micro-shadows cast along deeply ripped paper edges, exposed adhesive residue, and a gritty, analog punk-zine aesthetic.
