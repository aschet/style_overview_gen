# PREFIX
Composition: A waist up portrait of an Italian woman in her forties seated at a wooden dining table, her body angled slightly toward the viewer. She has long black hair falling over her shoulders and wears a plain T-shirt. Her expression is calm and neutral as she drinks from a glass of beer. Her posture is relaxed and natural.

# Painting: Oil Paint
Medium: Oil painting with slow-drying pigment layers enabling visible brushwork, wet-on-wet blending, glazing transitions, and thick-to-thin paint variation creating tactile surface depth on canvas.

# Painting: Watercolor
Medium: Watercolor painting with highly transparent pigment suspended in water, controlled diffusion into absorbent paper fibers, pigment blooms, backruns, and soft edge bleeding driven by fluid flow.

# Painting: Gouache
Medium: Gouache painting with opaque water-based pigment forming matte, flat color fields, minimal transparency, strong edge control, and layered coverage without visible substrate interaction.

# Painting: Encaustic
Medium: Encaustic painting using heated pigmented wax layered and fused into textured surfaces, producing translucent depth, embedded pigment strata, and thermally irregular surface variation.

# Drawing: Ink
Medium: Ink drawing in monochrome using liquid ink deposited via pen or brush, producing permanent high-contrast linework, pooling variation, and controlled bleed into paper fibers.

# Drawing: Charcoal
Medium: Charcoal drawing in monochrome using dry carbon particulate applied to paper surface, producing soft compressible tonal gradients, smudging behavior, and easily erasable layering.

# Drawing: Colored Pencil
Medium: Colored pencil drawing using wax or oil-bound pigment applied in directional strokes, producing layered optical color mixing, fine edge control, and visible stroke directionality.

# Drawing: Pastel
Medium: Pastel drawing using dry pigment sticks that deposit loose particulate color onto paper surface, producing powdery texture, high chroma layering, and minimal binder cohesion.

# Printmaking: Woodcut
Medium: Woodcut print in monochrome produced by relief carving into wood blocks, where raised surfaces transfer ink under pressure, creating bold high-contrast edges and visible wood grain interference.

# Printmaking: Lithograph
Medium: Lithograph print produced through oil–water repulsion on treated stone or plate, enabling continuous tonal gradation and ink retention in hydrophobic drawn regions.

# Printmaking: Screenprint
Medium: Screenprint using stencil-based ink transfer through mesh screens, producing flat color layers, hard edge boundaries, and mechanically separated tonal regions.

# Printmaking: Monotype
Medium: Monotype print created by applying ink to a smooth plate and transferring a single impression under pressure, producing painterly irregularity and non-reproducible textures.

# Photography: Gelatin Silver
Medium: Gelatin silver photograph in black and white produced via light exposure on silver halide emulsion, yielding continuous tonal range, deep blacks, and chemically developed grain structure.

# Photography: Chromogenic
Medium: Chromogenic color photograph produced via color dye development in layered emulsion, yielding natural color reproduction, smooth gradients, and fine photographic grain.

# Digital: Vector Graphic
Medium: Vector graphic generated through mathematical paths and geometric primitives, producing perfectly scalable forms, uniform fills, and resolution-independent crisp edges.

# Mixed Media: Collage
Medium: Mixed media collage constructed through physical assembly of heterogeneous materials such as cut or torn paper, adhesives, and layered surfaces, producing visible seams, overlaps, and material discontinuity.
